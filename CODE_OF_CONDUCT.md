# Tech of Our Own Code of Conduct

Tech of Our Own exists to build trustworthy technology and a trustworthy commons.
That requires a community where people can participate without fear of harassment,
demeaning behavior, or intimidation.

This Code of Conduct applies across all Tech of Our Own spaces, including all
repositories in the `techofourown` GitHub organization.

## 1) Our standards

We will:

- Treat people with respect, patience, and good faith.
- Assume competence and sincere intent unless there is clear evidence otherwise.
- Give feedback that is specific, constructive, and oriented toward the work.
- Protect privacy: do not pressure anyone to reveal personal information.
- Prefer clarity over sarcasm; prefer questions over dunking.
- Respect boundaries and consent.

## 2) Unacceptable behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- Harassment, discrimination, or hateful conduct (including slurs).
- Sexual harassment or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Threats of violence, intimidation, or encouragement of self-harm.
- Doxxing or sharing personal information without explicit permission.
- Persistent bad-faith disruption: trolling, sealioning, derailing, or targeted hostility.
- Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith.

## 3) Scope

This policy applies to:

- All repositories in the `techofourown` GitHub organization
- Pull requests, issues, code review, comments, and discussions in any repo
- Any official Tech of Our Own community space or event (online or in person)

## 4) Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, report it privately:

- Email: conduct@techofourown.org

If you do not have access to email or need an alternative channel, contact a repository maintainer
and ask for a private reporting path.

We will take reports seriously. We will respect confidentiality to the extent we can, while still
acting to keep the community safe.

## 5) Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

Responses may include:

1. **Correction** (clear request to stop / change behavior)
2. **Warning** (behavior must stop to continue participating)
3. **Temporary restriction** (cooldown period; limited participation)
4. **Removal** (ban from one or more spaces)

The response will be proportional to impact, pattern, and severity. Good-faith mistakes will be
treated differently from repeated or malicious behavior.

## 6) No retaliation

Retaliation against reporters or people assisting with enforcement is itself a violation.

## 7) Attribution

This policy is informed by common open-source community standards, including the Contributor Covenant,
but is written as a Tech of Our Own policy document.
